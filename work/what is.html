Communication at light speed

We invented fiber optic cable and this transmits data at near the speed of light. That's a 186,000 miles per second and it is the universal speed limit so now it's not so much about speed, it's about throughput. Throughput refers to how much data can be transferred from one location to another in a given amount of time. That's how much data you can shove through a length of cable at a given amount of time. And we've gotten really good at that too.
In 2012,communications companies got together and built a 12-core fiber optic cable capable of carrying one petabit of information per second across 30 miles.
